Pad Thai Chicken Recipe

Ingredients

  • 125 g / 4oz Chang’s Pad Thai dried rice stick
  • 1 1/2 tbsp tamarind puree
  • 3 tbsp (packed) brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 1/2 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 2 – 3 tbsp vegetable or canola oil
  • 1/2 onion
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 150 g/5oz chicken breast (or thigh)
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups of beansprouts
  • 1/2 cup firm tofu, cut into 3cm / 1 1/4″ batons
  • 1/4 cup garlic chives , cut into 3cm / 1 1/4″ pieces
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped peanuts

    Method

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the noodles and a big amount of boiling water. Soak for 5 minutes, then drain and rinse under cold water in a colander. Do not leave them to sit for longer than 5–10 minutes.

  2. Mix Sauce in small bowl.

  3. In a large nonstick skillet (or well-seasoned skillet), heat 2 tablespoons oil over high heat. Cook for 30 seconds after adding the garlic and onion.

  4. Cook for 1 1/2 minutes, or until chicken is almost cooked through.

  5. Push to one side of the pan, then pour in the egg on the other. Using the wooden spoon, scramble the eggs (adding a touch of more oil if the pan is too dry), then fold into the chicken.

  6. Add bean sprouts, tofu, noodles then Sauce.

  7. Toss for about 1 1/2 minutes, or until the sauce is completely absorbed by the noodles.

  8. Add half of the peanuts and the garlic chives. Remove from heat after a brief tossing.

  9. Serve immediately with a sprinkle of chili and a handful of additional beansprouts on the side, as well as the remaining peanuts and lime wedges. Before eating, squeeze over some lime juice to taste.
