Pineapple Chicken Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts cut into 1" pieces
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Pepper
  • 3 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 (8 fluid ounce) cans pineapple tidbits or chunks
  • 1/2 red bell pepper cut into bite-size pieces
  • 1/2 medium onion sliced or cut into bite-size pieces
    • Sauce:
  • 3/4 cup pineapple juice (from the canned pineapple)
  • 3 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon rice vinegar (or apple cider vinegar)
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger grated
  • 2 cloves garlic minced
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch

    Method

  1. In a small bowl, combine the sauce ingredients. If you don't have exactly enough pineapple juice to create 3/4 cup, simply add water to make up the difference. Whisk until the mixture is completely smooth.

  2. Prepare the veggies.

  3. Stir the chicken with the garlic powder and pepper in a bowl, then add the cornstarch and toss until evenly covered.

  4. In a deep pan, heat 2 tablespoons olive oil over medium-high heat. Allow a few minutes for the pan to heat up. Cook the chicken in two batches in order to avoid overcrowding the pan. Cook for 4 minutes before flipping and cooking for another 3-4 minutes. Brown the chicken and make sure it's fully done (165F). I add another tablespoon of olive oil to the pan for the second batch. When the chicken is done, transfer it to a platter in slices.

  5. If the pan is dry, drizzle with a tiny amount of olive oil (about a teaspoon) before adding the pineapple. Allow it to cook for approximately a minute without stirring.

  6. Cook for another 3 minutes, stirring often, after adding the peppers and onions. Tender-crisp vegetables will be served. If desired, cook for a few minutes longer.

  7. Return the chicken to the pan and then add the sauce. Allow it to boil for 1-2 minutes until it thickens, then remove from the heat and serve immediately over rice or noodles.
